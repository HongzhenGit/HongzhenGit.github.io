Published Papers

Extension of terahertz time-domain spectroscopy: A micron-level thickness gauging technology
with SHI Lili and HE Mingxia
Optics Communications, Published.

There is a minimum measurable thickness when gauging micron-level coating films with time-of-flight based terahertz thickness measurement technologies. To improve this minimum measurable limit, a time-domain model of terahertz signals is derived by taking multiple reflected pulses into consideration, using the Differential Evolution algorithm to calibrate parameters automatically. Empirical Mode Decomposition is applied to remove noises and non-flat baselines introduced by water vapor and other structure-borne noise resources. Combining methods mentioned above, thicknesses of micron-level coating layers could be measured even if the Signal to Noise Ratio is not large enough, and the minimum measurable limit is improved to about 40μm. Experiments also demonstrate that, during drying processes, the time gaps between pulses reflected from different interfaces shrink and the thicknesses change in an exponential trend, which reveals the potential of terahertz technologies in terms of monitoring coating processes.

The Application of Stochastic Optimization Algorithm in Terahertz Thickness Measurement Technology (in Chinese)
with HE Mingxia, SHI Lili and WANG Pengfei
Spectroscopy and Spectral Analysis, Published.

Coating is an indispensable process in automobile, marine, aerospace manufacturing and other industries. A reasonable film thickness is not only conducive to the stability of painting quality, but also conducive to saving painting materials and reducing painting cost. Compared with traditional measurement methods, time-of-flight based terahertz measurement method could perform non-contact, non-destructive and high-precision online detection. However, when the optical thickness of the sample is too small, terahertz reflection pulses will overlap in the time domain, which makes it hard to extract the flight time directly from the peak positions of reflected pulses. In order to solve this problem, a thickness measurement method is proposed based on reflective terahertz time domain spectroscopy and a stochastic optimization algorithm named Differential Evolution(DE). To evaluate the stability of our method, we calculated the measurement errors introduced by displacement angles between the normal direction of sample substrate and the direction of terahertz probe. In addition, the capability of time-of-flight (TOF) method for measuring the thickness of multi-layer structure samples is also studied. Results show that, the uncertainties when measuring thicknesses for zinc dipping paint, black paint and base paint are 0.22μm(223.87μm), 0.05μm(54.18μm) and 0.08μm(284.95μm), respectively. And for refractive indexes, they are 0.004(3.967), 002(2.091) and 0.001(1.769). In case of zinc dipping paint, a displacement angle of 1 leads to a measurement error of 0.073μm. When detecting multi-layer samples, although our method could get the flight time of each reflection pulse, it is impossible to distinguish which reflection interface the reflection pulse comes from, so that the thickness of each layer cannot be measured.
