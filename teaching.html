Feature Selection for Generalized Memory Polynomial(GMP) Model(ADI, 07.2019 - 01.2020)

Generalized Memory Polynomial(GMP) Model is a widely-used compensation structure in Digital Pre-Distortion. To get a desirable improvment in linearity, this GMP model could be very complex, which means, lots of circuit components need to be embeded on one chip(One feature in GMP needs a group of components). To reduce cost and simplify the structure of DPD, a good solution is to only keep those featrues that have significant impact on the compensation of linearity. However, DPD is working on the frequency domain, in other words, the data used to build GMP model are complex numbers, so it is necessary to extend some optimization and feature selection methods to make them available for complex numbers.
  • Gradient Descent and Gauss-Newton Method in the complex domain
  • LASSO regression in the complex domain
  • Research on LASSO solution path
  • A reference for complex lasso(Muhammad Naveed Tabassum and Esa Ollila)
